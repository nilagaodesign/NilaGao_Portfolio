Brand Voice

A foundational system that infers, configures, and applies brand tone across Email AI and Subject Line Assistant.

Brand Voice system overview

I led the 0→1 design of Brand Voice—a foundational system that infers, configures, and applies brand tone and writing patterns across Email AI and Subject Line Assistant.

The work focused on improving AI content acceptance and consistency through system and interaction design—rather than changes to the underlying model.

Role
Product Designer (AI)
Company
Klaviyo
Focus
System Design, Interaction Design, Trust

Background

Klaviyo lacked a way to apply brand voice to AI-generated content, resulting in heavily edited drafts, reduced trust, and low AI acceptance.

Business Problem

  • Low AI content acceptance limited return on AI investment
  • Manual editing reduced the efficiency gains AI was meant to deliver
  • Improving AI acceptance (~60% → ~70%) was a core company goal

User Problem

  • No clear way to define or apply brand voice to AI outputs
  • AI drafts often felt off-brand or inconsistent
  • High editing effort increased cognitive load and slowed workflows
  • Unclear inference reduced trust and repeat usage

Constraints

  • Brand voice is nuanced and difficult to codify
  • Inference depended on limited or variable historical data
  • The system needed to scale across multiple AI surfaces and future use cases

Design Thinking

My goal was to design and ship an end-to-end Brand Voice system that infers a customer's tone and writing patterns, allows marketers to review and refine them, and applies brand guidelines automatically across Email AI and Subject Line Assistant.

Customer Research

I studied how marketers maintain brand voice in practice, focusing on concrete signals rather than abstract guidelines. Brand voice shows up through tone, grammar, emoji usage, CTA patterns, audience alignment, and ongoing iteration based on performance.

Customer research / brand voice signals image

Concept Exploration

From this understanding, I explored three primary directions:

Concept 1 image

Streamlined Brand Upload

Users upload a brand PDF or document; AI extracts tone and missing attributes.

Pros: Fast setup, familiar pattern

Open questions: Is upload enough? What data is required for reliable inference?

Concept 2 image

Iterative Comparison

Side-by-side view of original content and AI-generated drafts with real-time updates.

Pros: Fast feedback loop

Tradeoff: Complexity and unclear prompting boundaries

Concept 3 image

In-Editor Personalization

Users directly rewrite AI-generated text or add context inline.

Pros: High control

Risk: High effort; unclear long-term value

Key Insight

Users wanted automation by default, control when needed, and transparency into why AI sounded the way it did.

Core principle: Brand voice should be inferred automatically, editable explicitly, and applied invisibly.

Final Design

I designed an end-to-end Brand Voice system integrated across Email AI and Subject Line Assistant.

Impact

Product & Business Impact

  • Established Brand Voice as a foundational layer of Klaviyo's AI strategy
  • Directly supports the 2025 goal of increasing AI content acceptance from ~60% → ~70%
  • Enabled consistent, scalable brand alignment across current and future AI surfaces

Challenges

  • Aligned Design, Data Science, and Engineering on system boundaries and ownership
  • Balanced transparency with automation to build trust without added friction

Reflection

Brand Voice reframed AI quality as a system problem—where trust, control, and consistency matter as much as model capability.

Background

The problem wasn’t the model — it was how AI behaved. When AI lived behind a disconnected modal, required heavy prompting, and produced generic outputs, adoption stayed low because the experience was hard to access, hard to trust, and removed from the writing flow.

What was broken

  • High friction: multi-step interactions made AI feel like extra work
  • Low relevance: outputs didn’t reflect what the marketer was writing right now
  • Low trust: users couldn’t tell why suggestions sounded the way they did
  • High edits: generic drafts required heavy rewriting to become usable

Constraints

  • Brand voice is nuanced and difficult to codify
  • Inference depended on limited or variable historical data
  • The system needed to scale across multiple AI surfaces and future use cases

Design Thinking

My goal was to improve AI adoption by designing interaction patterns that reduce friction, increase relevance, and create a measurable feedback loop.

Key insight

AI output quality is shaped by interaction design, not just model capability.

Users judge quality through flow, relevance, and how much editing outputs require—so the system has to show up at the right time, in the right place, in the right form.

Design heuristics

I turned “good AI behavior” into a reusable set of design rules.

Guidance without friction

AI assists within the workflow instead of requiring extra steps or explicit prompting.

Context-aware generation

Suggestions reflect what the user is writing and adapt to brand and campaign context.

Editable, not authoritative

AI behaves like a collaborator, giving users easy ways to accept, refine, or ignore outputs.

Low interruption

Suggestions appear at the right moment without blocking manual writing or breaking flow.

Transparent intelligence

Users can understand why outputs look the way they do, increasing trust and repeat usage.

Graceful fallback

When data is limited, the system sets expectations clearly and still provides useful assistance.

Evaluation framework

I built a quality framework to evaluate AI beyond novelty.

What defines quality
  • Relevance
  • Clarity
  • Brand alignment
  • Actionability
How we measure it
  • Acceptance rate
  • Edit distance
  • Engagement
  • Repeat usage

Experimentation

I tested which interaction pattern actually improved adoption and trust.

Modal
  • Disconnected from workflow
  • High friction
  • Low iteration
Reactive
  • Button-triggered
  • Optional but delayed
  • Still added effort
Inline · Winner
  • Real-time and contextual
  • Lower friction
  • Highest adoption

Result: Proactive inline AI drove approximately 6× higher message-level adoption and 8× higher account-level adoption, validating interaction design as the lever for unlocking meaningful AI usage.
